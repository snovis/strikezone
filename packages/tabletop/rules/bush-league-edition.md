## Game Flow

### 1. SELECT
Both players secretly choose their pitch/swing approach using Rock-Paper-Scissors:

| Hand | Pitch | Why It Makes Sense |
|------|-------|-------------------|

### 2. REVEAL
Both players throw their hands simultaneously.

### 3. CALC MODS
- **RPS Winner:** Gets +2 to their battle roll
- **RPS Tie:** No modifier for either player

### 4. BATTLE
Both players roll 2d6 + modifiers. Higher total wins the at-bat.
- **Ties:** Re-roll until someone wins

**Important:** The winner's battle roll determines their **OFFSET** for the result:
- Weak (‚â§6): Offset 0
- Solid (7-9): Offset 1
- Strong (10+): Offset 2

### 5. RESULT
The winner rolls 2d6 again. This determines which outcome they get from their offset window.

## Outcome Definitions

### Batter Outcomes
| Code | Result |
|------|--------|
| OUT | Batter is out |
| 1B | Single - batter to first |
| 2B | Double - batter to second |
| HR | Home Run - batter scores, all runners score |

### Pitcher Outcomes
| Code | Result | Runner Effect |
|------|--------|---------------|
| BB | Walk | Batter to 1B, forced runners advance |
| O-RA | Out, Runners Advance | Sac fly, grounder to right side - runners move up one base |
| O-RC | Out, Runner Challenge | Runner may attempt to advance - if they go, it's a 50/50 battle roll |
| O-RF | Out, Runners Freeze | No runner advancement allowed |
| DP | Double Play | 2 outs (any runners), players narrate the story |

---

## Runner Challenge (O-RC) Resolution
When O-RC occurs and a runner decides to go:
1. Both players roll 2d6 (no modifiers)
2. Higher roll wins
3. **Runner wins:** Safe at next base
4. **Defense wins:** Runner is out
5. **Tie:** Re-roll

## Design Notes

**Cheddar Bob: Bush League** strips the game down to pure competition. No fancy equipment, no complex rules - just read your opponent and roll.

The magic number **7-9** (the "strike zone") is the only range you need to remember - it applies to both battle rolls and result rolls.

The RPS pitch selection maps intuitively:
- **Rock** is hard and fast (fastball)
- **Paper** floats (changeup)
- **Scissors** cut and move (breaking ball)

Every at-bat tells a story. The outcome codes give players narrative freedom to describe what happened.

## Critical Rolls

- **Snake Eyes (2):** Critical Fail - worst outcome for the roller
  - Batter wins battle, rolls snake eyes ‚Üí OUT (snatched defeat from victory)
  - Pitcher wins battle, rolls snake eyes ‚Üí BB (plunked the batter!)

- **Boxcars (12):** Critical Hit - best outcome for the roller
  - Batter wins battle, rolls boxcars ‚Üí HR (crushed it!)
  - Pitcher wins battle, rolls boxcars ‚Üí DP (turned two!)
